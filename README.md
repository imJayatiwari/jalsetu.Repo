# JalSetu — Community Water Sharing & Tracking Network

**Theme:** DHARTI (धरती) — Climate, Environment & Ecological Resilience
**Sub-theme:** Water scarcity & access
**Hackathon:** Build for Good 2026

## The Problem

In many small towns and villages across India, when households face water shortages, they informally borrow water from neighbours who have functional borewells, tankers, or wells. This happens entirely through word-of-mouth — with no record of who borrowed what, leading to:

- Disputes between neighbours over unreturned favours
- Unequal access, since people without strong social ties get left out
- Wasted time and effort searching for working water sources during peak scarcity (especially summer)
- No visibility for local governments into which areas face chronic water stress

## The Solution

JalSetu (Hindi: "water bridge") is a lightweight web platform with two core features:

1. **Water Ledger** — A transparent, shared record of water lent and borrowed between neighbours, similar to a simple IOU system. Reduces disputes and encourages fair reciprocity.

2. **Live Source Map** — A crowdsourced, real-time view of which water sources (borewells, tankers, community wells) are currently functional, running low, or dry — reported by the community itself.

## Why It's Different

Most water-tech solutions stop at "find water nearby." JalSetu instead formalizes the **social trust and lending dynamics** that already exist informally in water-stressed communities — turning an invisible, dispute-prone system into a transparent, fair one.

## Features

- 📍 Crowdsourced water source status map (functional / low / dry)
- 💧 Personal water ledger with running balance
- 🪣 Simple lend/borrow logging — no technical literacy required
- 🌐 Designed for low-bandwidth, low-smartphone-literacy environments

## Tech Stack

- HTML, CSS, JavaScript (vanilla, no framework dependencies — keeps it lightweight for low-end devices)
- Designed to integrate with SMS/WhatsApp reporting in future versions for non-smartphone users
